After mastering the fundamentals and you are able to transform from a digital circuit schematic to a Verilog code, and vice-versa, the answer is YES.

You should be able to apply at least for an internship for Digital Design or Functional Verification based on Verilog. This course contains many industry-level best practices that are not emphasized / presented at academic level.


Also the proficiency in working with Modelsim is very useful to a job interview. Most industry simulators are similar with and you will become familiar with them easier at the job.


If you want to do this I advise you to write all the Verilog code lines to gain experience writing code and simulate it using Modelsim. This will give you hands-on experience that you will use in more complex projects at your future job.
